# Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how employees and other authorized users may use [Company Name] devices, systems, networks and data.

It exists to protect the organization, our customers and our partners from misuse, data loss and security incidents.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, interns and temporary workers.
- Any person who has access to [Company Name] systems, networks or data, whether on-site or remote.

It covers company-owned devices and services, as well as personal devices when they are used to access company systems or data (for example, email on a personal phone).

## 3. General acceptable use

- Company devices and accounts are provided primarily for **business use**.
- Limited personal use is acceptable if it:
  - Does not interfere with work duties.
  - Does not violate any law or company policy.
  - Does not create security risks for [Company Name].

Users are expected to exercise good judgment and professional behavior when using company resources.

## 4. Prohibited activities

Users must not:

- Install software, browser extensions or apps on company devices without approval from [IT / Security / System Owner].
- Disable, bypass or tamper with security controls (for example, antivirus, firewall, automatic updates, content filters, MFA).
- Use company systems for illegal activities, harassment, discrimination, or any form of abusive behavior.
- Store company data in unapproved locations (for example, personal cloud storage, personal email accounts, personal USB drives) without written approval.
- Share company data with unauthorized individuals or on public platforms.

## 5. User responsibilities

Users must:

- Use strong, unique passwords or passphrases and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) where required.
- Lock their screen or log out when leaving a device unattended.
- Keep devices physically secure (for example, not leaving laptops in unlocked cars or public places).
- Immediately report lost or stolen devices and suspected security incidents to [Contact / Team].
- Follow any additional technical guidelines issued by [IT / Security].

## 6. Monitoring and enforcement

[Company Name] may monitor the use of its systems and networks, in accordance with applicable laws and internal policies.

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, and may involve legal action where appropriate.

## 7. Review

This policy should be reviewed at least annually, or when significant changes occur in the organization’s technology, risk profile or legal requirements.

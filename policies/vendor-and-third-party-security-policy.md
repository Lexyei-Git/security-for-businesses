# Vendor and Third-Party Security Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets minimum security expectations for vendors and other third parties that store, process or have access to [Company Name] systems or data.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Cloud services and SaaS providers.
- Managed service providers (MSPs and MSSPs).
- Contractors and partners with access to internal systems or sensitive data.

## 3. Approval of new vendors

Staff must not engage new vendors that:

- Store or process customer, employee or sensitive business data, or
- Have remote or administrative access to [Company Name] systems,

without approval from [Owner / Security / IT / Management].

## 4. Security due diligence

For higher-risk vendors, [Owner / Team] must perform basic due diligence, which may include:

- Asking about:
  - Use of MFA for staff and admin accounts.
  - Data protection measures (encryption, access control, backups).
  - Incident response and breach notification procedures.
  - Relevant certifications or assessments (for example, SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI DSS where applicable).

- Reviewing:
  - Security summaries or whitepapers.
  - Contractual terms related to security and privacy.

## 5. Contractual expectations

Where feasible, contracts with higher-risk vendors should:

- Require reasonable protections for [Company Name] data.
- Require timely notification of security incidents affecting [Company Name].
- Define how data will be returned or securely destroyed at the end of the relationship.

## 6. Ongoing monitoring

[Owner / Team] should:

- Maintain a list of key vendors and their access to systems and data.
- Periodically (for example, annually) review high-risk vendors for major changes, incidents or issues.
- Reassess risk when vendors gain new access or take on new responsibilities.

## 7. Review

This policy should be reviewed at least annually, or when significant changes occur in the vendor landscape or regulatory environment.

# Backup and Data Protection Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes how [Company Name] backs up and protects critical data so that it can be recovered in the event of a security incident, system failure or human error.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All systems and applications identified as critical or important for business operations.
- All data classified as sensitive or business-critical.

## 3. Responsibilities

- [Owner/Role] is responsible for defining which systems and data are in scope for backups.
- [IT / Service Provider] is responsible for implementing, monitoring and testing backups.
- Business owners (for example, Finance, HR, Sales) are responsible for confirming that backups meet their operational needs.

## 4. Backup requirements

[Company Name] will:

- Maintain regular backups of critical systems and data, with a frequency appropriate to business needs (for example, daily).
- Keep at least one backup copy **separate** from the primary environment (for example, off-site or in the cloud).
- Ensure backups are **encrypted** and access is limited to authorized personnel.
- Retain backups for a period that supports business, legal and regulatory requirements.

## 5. Restore testing

- Backups must be **tested periodically** (for example, quarterly) by restoring selected files or systems.
- Test results must be documented, including:
  - What was restored.
  - How long it took.
  - Any issues identified and corrective actions.

## 6. User responsibilities

Users must:

- Store important work in approved locations (for example, company file servers, approved cloud storage), not only on local devices.
- Not create unofficial or personal backups of company data in unapproved locations.

## 7. Review

This policy should be reviewed at least annually or when major changes occur in systems, data classification or business continuity requirements.
